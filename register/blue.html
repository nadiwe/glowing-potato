_CUT_OUT

_modul

ZHdK | IAD | Soft Architecture HS21
Group Work


_about

As part of the module, we dealt with impulses that are evoked in us by the environment or objects around us. One such impulse was a trench we found in an abandoned military installation in Pula, Cro- atia. In the second part of the module, we got to adapt the project to our school environment. The tower-staircase in ZHdK inspired our vision. A place that is usually used for transfer was given a new meaning by placing 3D objects into a digital space. This AR experience offered a space for playfulness and curiosity . The visitors attempted to look at the objects from different perspec- tives and at the same time discovered the different characters of the forms through their movement. this behavior of the visitor led to a new animation and application of the staircase.


_focus

_transforming a physical concept (Pula) into a digital one (Zürich) _bringing digital and pysical room together
_creating an exhibition exerience
_finding the right programs for our needs : Reality Composer, Adobe After Effects, Blender, Adobe Aero
_what gives us space? Do we shape it? Or does it shape us?
_do we adapt to it or do we develop it?

_POOYA

_modul

ZHdK | IAD | Interaction Design Process FS21
Group Work


_about

Pooya is an autonomous robot that humans can interact with by bringing their bodies close to it. This way Pooyas path can be al- tered and even controlled. This way Pooya leaves behind a trace of it journey and the encounters it had along the way. The chalk trail was chosen for its temporary quality and contrasting color to the concrete surface as well as for its playful character. Pooya’s col- lected visual impressions combined with the corresponding sound- scapes are recorded and made accessible on a website.


_focus

_how can we communicate a non-human viewpoint of the world?
_how are spontaneous encounters between humans and non-humans created?
_how can we make these non-tangible encounters visible?
_working in a group over a longer period of time using a design thinking process
_creating a website and linking to github _arduino wiring and code writing

_FEEDBACK_OF_LIGHT

_modul

ZHdK | Interdisciplinary | Color, Light & Interaction FS21
Group Work


_about

Immerse yourself in a colorful world. This immersive light installation plays with projecting and splitting of light. Through a mapping program (MadMapper) a rectangle, which changes dimension and colors, is created in the front of the scene. panels are attached to both sides of the scren area. A shift- ing color gradient can be seen on them. This color gradient is creat- ed by a camera shot of the mapped rectangles. This color spectrum is created by a discrepancy in the shutter speed and frame rate of the camera. These settings cause the captured light source to split into its existing primary colors of the additive color mixing.


_focus

_technical aspect: working with several beamers, cameras and the mapping program MadMapper
_experimenting with video feedback
_designing a room with light phenomenon
_explore additive color mixing through frame rate settings

Florian Bachmann and Marcus Pericin


_students

_OHRBIT

_modul

ZHdK | IAD | Sonic Interaction HS21
Group Work


_about

Ohrbit is an interactive, spatial and immersive sound installation. Consisting of a portable sphere, a pedestal and four speakers, a unique sound experience is created. By interacting with the ped- estal or rotating the sphere in space, the user can explore the soundscape around them.The installation is divided into two sound worlds. They are fundamentally opposed to each other. If the sphere is on the pedestal, the inside of the sphere is explored. If the sphere is in space, the soundscape opens up in a room experience.


_focus

_OCS connecting with processing and ableton live
_getting an understanding of how sounds effect emotions
_how to create sound interaction between space, object and person

_students

_I'M_SIMPLE

_modul

ZHdK | IAD | Bits & Atoms II FS21


_about

As part of my essay „ecological sustainability in digital design“, I was thinking about the effects of web processes on the environ- ment. I asked myself what knowledge already exists and how this can be incorporated into a working prototype. Projects like the web- site of Kris De Decker “LOW TECH MAGAZINE”, the book of Tom Greenwood “Sustainable Web Design” or the documentation of an Amazon order by the artist Joana Moll , inspired me to dedicate myself to the topic.Therefore decided to challenge myself al little bit and create an HTML file that would be as small as possible. If the website would run over a server, I would have added a gzip compression, which would lower its impact even further.


_focus

_generating as few bits as possible
_create a light point that gives insight into background
